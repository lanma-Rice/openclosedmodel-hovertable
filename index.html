Open vs. Closed AI Models
Aspect Open Models Closed Models
Source Code Available Source code is publicly available, allowing inspection, modification, and distribution. Source code is proprietary and not available to the public.
Customization High level of customization, enabling tailored solutions. Limited customization to vendor-provided features.
Community Support Strong community support with forums, user groups, and extensive documentation. Support limited to what the vendor provides.
Security Security through transparency, with vulnerabilities addressed by the community. Security is opaque, relying on the vendor.
Cost Often free to use, with no licensing fees. Cost varies, often involving licensing fees and subscription costs.
Flexibility High flexibility due to modifiability and extendability. Lower flexibility as users are confined to vendor functionalities.
